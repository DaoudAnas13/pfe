\chapter{Analysis and Requirements Specification}

\section{Introduction}

This chapter formalizes the requirements engineering process of RM by transforming the PRD into structured, verifiable, and implementable specifications. It identifies actors, role codes, functional and non-functional requirements, and use cases. It also introduces global interaction views, interface prototyping placeholders, and SCRUM management artifacts used to control incremental delivery.

\section{Capturing Needs}

\subsection{Actors Identification}

The business workflow relies on four core actors and one external stakeholder.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{|l|l|X|}
\hline

\hline
Field Technician & ROLE\_TECHNICIAN & Execute interventions, complete dynamic forms, attach files, capture geolocation, submit execution details \\
\hline
Project Supervisor & ROLE\_SUPERVISOR & Review submissions, approve/reject interventions, provide rejection motifs and feedback, track pending validations \\
\hline
Project Coordinator & ROLE\_COORDINATOR & Create requests and templates, configure export mapping, generate and download compliance reports \\
\hline
System Administrator & ROLE\_ADMIN & Manage users and permissions, enforce security policies, supervise platform configuration and auditing \\

Telecom Operator (External) & EXTERNAL\_CONSUMER & Consume generated reports in standardized Excel format for contractual compliance \\

\end{table}
